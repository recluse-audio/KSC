#### Love of my life

I fell in love in grad school. 
It was the first and only time it has ever happened. 
Tough to say.

In measure of my feelings, it was the truest. 
But I'm not sure that is a high bar to clear.

I fell in love because I had decided to fall in love. 
I had made the conscious decision to go
gung-ho after ending of previous 
dispassionate entanglements. 
It proved to me that love is the commitment. 
The resignment of yourself to the inevitable defeat 
that can only end in heartbreak or one of you dying.

Though my entrance was arguably arbitrary, 
my exit was catastrophic. 
It ended after 2 months, and I was devastated.
Why would I be so attached? And why her? 
She's a lovely girl,
 but surely I've had women with whom I'm more well suited.

The closer I feel to them, the less I can love them.
 That is not true, but more so that I cannot MAKE love to them.
The familiarity becomes a chore, 
meanwhile my partners were adoring me 
more and more like a song that you kind of like at first and
comes to dominate an era of your existence.

So when my first and only love had come and passed, 
I began a transformation.
Into something that can't feel beyond a reasonable amount
of emotional investment equivalent to that which 
would arise in 2 months of partnership for most couples.

Code doesn't do that to you, 
it doesnt' leave you feeling as though 
everything shifted around you without you realizing it.
If it does, you can retrace it to why it is the way it is. 
You can argue over changing it or not, 
but it exists in a state and 
can be referenced as good, bad, or neutral.

These fucking black box llms shit all over that idea. 
I am fearful that the mystery of it all will 
instantiate in a way that destroys what you think.

Well anyways, I found a new girl lol. 
This is what made me think of old Meredith. 
This new girl is something special. 
A total sweetheart.
